About Food For All


The Food For All program is an initiative that aims to tackle a global problem: hunger. Across the world almost 9% of the population goes hungry which equates to roughly 690 million people per day. Numerous factors such as economic position or location are attributed to this alarming statisic. Thankfully, due to the generous and life-saving services provided by food banks, people are able to get quality meals on their tables. However, many donors cannot donate these foods due to them not knowing where their nearest foodbank is. Our product aims to tackle this issue and help families get food on their tables.


Our Product


The FFA food-donor application is a Javascript-based application that allows people to look for foodbanks in the Bay Area. We wanted to incorporate elements of datasets into our project and build something that would solve a problem since we that was covered in our engineering class: AP Computer Science. Though the product currently is strictly limited to the Bay Area, it shows how useful the product can be when give more information. Currently, our product is fully operational but we plan on making it more user-friendly (through the creation of an app). If you scroll to the "Donate" page on this website, you can try out our product as well.
